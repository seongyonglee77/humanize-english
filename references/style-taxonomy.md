# Academic Style Taxonomy

## Overview

This taxonomy organizes writing style rules into categories by priority and impact.

## Category Definitions

### Category A: Word Formation
**Priority: HIGH**

Rules affecting how words are constructed and combined. These have the most immediate impact on readability and "AI-generated" feel.

**Principles:**
- Avoid compound labels that pack too much meaning
- Use "of" construction for non-human possession
- Prefer descriptive phrases over condensed compounds
- **Preserve hyphens that clarify meaning**: `AI-assisted`, `well-known`, `decision-making`, `teacher-reported`, `pre-service`

**Common Violations:**
- AI-generated → materials generated with AI (informal compound label)
- GenAI's role → the role of GenAI
- teacher-led → led by teachers

**Exceptions:**
- Hyphens that clarify meaning are correct and should be kept
- En-dashes (–) for ranges (2022–2023) and relationships (parent–child) are correct

---

### Category B: Word Choice
**Priority: MEDIUM**

Rules about specific word selections. These improve conciseness and reduce "padding."

**Principles:**
- Remove words that don't add meaning
- Use simple words over formal/Latin vocabulary
- Avoid turning verbs into nouns (nominalization)
- **Do NOT blanket-remove `often`, `also`, or `both`** — they serve legitimate logical and emphatic functions when contextually appropriate

**Common Violations:**
- various dimensions → dimensions
- the articulation of → describing
- via → using

---

### Category C: Sentence Structure
**Priority: MEDIUM**

Rules about how sentences are constructed. These improve flow and clarity.

**Principles:**
- Start with subject, not subordinate clauses
- Use finite verbs instead of participles
- Prefer active voice
- Avoid unnecessary editorializing

**Common Violations:**
- While X..., Y → X..., but Y
- with X doing Y → while X does Y
- It is recommended → Please/Researchers should

---

### Category D: Punctuation & Flow
**Priority: LOW**

Rules about punctuation and citation formatting. These have subtle effects on readability.

**Principles:**
- Avoid excessive em-dashes (—); use standard punctuation instead
- Distinguish em-dash (—) from en-dash (–) — en-dashes for ranges/relationships are correct
- Place citations at sentence ends when possible

**Common Violations:**
- X—Y—Z → X (Y) Z (em-dash overuse)
- (Author, year) mid-sentence → at end

**Exceptions:**
- En-dashes (–) in ranges (2022–2023) and relationships (parent–child) are correct and should not be flagged

---

## Severity Levels

### S1: Critical (Always Fix)
- A-1: Compound words (teacher-led, AI-generated)
- A-2: Non-human possessive (GenAI's, technology's)
- C-2: "with ~ing" constructions

### S2: Important (Usually Fix)
- B-1: Filler words (various, significant)
- C-1: "While" at sentence start
- B-2: Formal vocabulary (via, rationale)

### S3: Optional (Context-Dependent)
- D-1: Em-dashes (if used for emphasis)
- D-2: Citation placement (if clarity requires mid-sentence)
- C-3: Active voice (if passive is standard in field)

---

## Application Order

### Pass 1: HIGH Priority (A Category)
Fix all S1 violations first. These make the biggest difference.

### Pass 2: MEDIUM Priority (B & C Categories)
Fix S2 violations. Check for S3 if time permits.

### Pass 3: LOW Priority (D Category)
Fix remaining S3 violations last.

---

## Exceptions & Context

### Abstracts
Some rules may be relaxed in abstracts:
- B-3 nominalizations may be acceptable for density
- C-3 passive voice may be preferred for objectivity

### Direct Quotes
Never apply rules inside quotation marks.

### Field-Specific Terms
Some "formal" vocabulary may be standard in specific fields:
- "post hoc" in statistics
- "regimes" in political science
- "competencies" in education (when plural is meaningful)

### Data/Numbers
Never change:
- Numerical values
- Statistical terms
- Citation formats (only placement)

---

## Validation Checklist

After applying rules, verify:

- [ ] No data or numbers changed
- [ ] All citations preserved
- [ ] Academic tone maintained (not too casual)
- [ ] Meaning preserved in all changes
- [ ] Rules applied consistently throughout
- [ ] No new errors introduced
